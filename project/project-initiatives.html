Balcony-to-Table Challenge I

Re-grow Lettuce from Scraps (No Seeds Needed!)

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️:

  • Need 1: The bottom 1–2 inches of a romaine (or butter, bibb) lettuce head.
  • Need 2: A shallow bowl or small dish.
  • Need 3: Water (tap is fine).
  • Need 4: Sunny windowsill or balcony or kitchen counter.
  • Need 5: Optional: Pot + soil for transplanting later.
  • Step 1: Cut Your Lettuce 🔪 Eat/use most of your lettuce and leave about 2 inches of the base intact.
  • Step 2: Set It in Water 💧 Place the base in a shallow bowl with about ½ inch of water,let the cut side face up.
  • Step 3: Find Some Sun ☀️ Put the bowl near a sunny window or bright spot. Change the water every 1–2 days to prevent slime and mold.
  • Step 4: Watch It Grow! 🌱 Within 2–3 days, you’ll see new leaves sprouting from the center. In 5–7 days, you’ll have 1–3 inches of regrowth!
  • Step 5: Transplant (Optional) 🌿 Once new roots or tall leaves develop, move it into a pot with soil. Keep watering and watch it grow fuller. Harvest when leaves are 4–6 inches tall.

Tips 💡Don’t submerge the top — it should sit just above waterline. Use filtered/rainwater if your tap water has lots of chlorine.

Compost Across Cities

Start Your Own Balcony Compost Bin

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️🔁:

  • Need 1: A plastic container or old bucket with a lid (5–10 liters is enough).
  • Need 2: A drill or nail to make holes (for airflow).
  • Need 3: Dry leaves, shredded newspaper, or cardboard (carbon).
  • Need 4: Fruit & veggie peels, coffee grounds, eggshells (nitrogen).
  • Need 5: A little garden soil (to introduce microbes).
  • Need 6: A stick or old spoon for mixing.
  • Step 1: Drill air holes around the lid and upper sides of the container.
  • Step 2: Add a layer of dry brown materials at the bottom (shredded paper/leaves).
  • Step 3: Layer your kitchen scraps on top (green matter). Sprinkle a handful of soil to kickstart decomposition.
  • Step 4: Alternate layers every few days and mix the contents once a week.
  • Step 5: In 4–8 weeks, you'll have rich compost to use in your urban garden pots!

Tips 💡Keep the bin slightly moist but not soggy.

Share Your Progress 📸Take “before & after” photos of their compost bin. Record weekly progress (smell, texture, what's breaking down fastest). Share creative ways they decorated their compost bins.

Rooftop Pollinator Gardens

Bring the Buzz to the Roof

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️:

  • Need 1: Large containers or grow bags. Old buckets, crates, or pots work great.
  • Need 2: Potting mix with compost. Lightweight is best for rooftops.
  • Need 3: Native flowering plants Think milkweed, coneflowers, lavender, marigold, salvia.
  • Need 4: Water source. A shallow dish with stones for bees to land on.
  • Need 5: (Optional) Upcycled bee hotel- Made from bamboo sticks, wood blocks, or even drilled bricks.
  • Need 6: Mulch - Helps retain moisture and protect roots.
  • Step 1: Plan the space. Pick a sunny spot on your rooftop or terrace with at least 6–8 hours of sunlight.
  • Step 2: Select plants for pollinators. Go for a mix of colors and bloom times. Use native plants when possible. Include: Early bloomers (like crocuses), Mid-season (lavender, marigolds), Late bloomers (asters, goldenrod).
  • Step 3: Build the containers. Fill them with compost-rich soil. Add drainage holes if needed.
  • Step 4: Create bee water station. Place a shallow dish with pebbles and water — pollinators need to drink too!
  • Step 5: Avoid pesticides. Pollinator gardens should stay organic. Use natural pest control like neem or companion planting.
  • Step 6: (Optional) DIY Bee Hotel - made with bundle bamboo sticks or drill holes into wood blocks to give solitary bees a nesting spot.

Tips 💡Mix heights and colors to attract more pollinators. Keep flowering plants close together to make it easier for pollinators to hop between them. Try adding night-blooming plants to attract moths too!

Share Your Progress 📸Show before/after photos. Track pollinators visiting their garden (bees, butterflies, moths, even hummingbirds!). Share one fun fact about a plant or pollinator they observed.

Upcycled Vertical Garden Challenge

Edible/ Ornamental Garden Wall

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️:

  • Need 1: Plastic bottles, tin cans, shoe organizers, old gutters, or pallets.
  • Need 2: Zip ties, string, hooks, or wire.
  • Need 3: Scissors, hammer/nails, or a drill (depending on your materials).
  • Need 4: Potting soil + compost.
  • Need 5: Easy-to-grow plants: mint, basil, lettuce, succulents, strawberries,cabbage,spring onions.
  • Step 1: Pick your structure – a wall, balcony railing, fence, or trellis.
  • Step 2: Prep your planters – cut bottles/cans, poke drainage holes, paint or decorate!
  • Step 3: Attach vertically using hooks, zip ties, or nails.
  • Step 4: Add soil and plant your chosen herbs or flowers.
  • Step 5: Water from the top—let it trickle down!

Tips 💡Label your plants with hand-painted sticks or corks. Use drip irrigation from an old water bottle to keep it green with less waste!

Balcony-to-Table Challenge II

Re-grow Basil & Mint from Cuttings

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️:

  • Need 1: A healthy stem of basil or mint (4–6 inches long) gotten from the grocery store or garden cuttings — no seeds, just stems!
  • Need 2: A small glass or jar of water.
  • Need 3: Sunny windowsill or balcony.
  • Need 4: (Optional) Small pot + soil for transplanting after rooting.
  • Step 1: Snip the Cutting, Cut ✂️ a 4–6 inch stem from a healthy plant. Remove the bottom leaves, leaving just a few at the top.
  • Step 2: Place in Water 💧 Put the stem in a glass of water — only the stem should be submerged, not the leaves. Use a clear container so you can watch the roots grow.
  • Step 3: Find a Sunny Spot ☀️ Place the jar near a bright window (indirect light works well too). Change the water every 2–3 days to keep it fresh.
  • Step 4: Wait for Roots 🌱 In 5–10 days, you’ll start to see roots forming! Once roots are about 1–2 inches long, you can transplant into soil.
  • Step 5: Transplant (Optional) 🌿 Use a small pot with rich, well-draining soil. Water it gently and keep in a warm, bright spot.

Tips 💡Don’t submerge the top — it should sit just above waterline. Use filtered/rainwater if your tap water has lots of chlorine.

Share Your Progress 📸Basil loves sun ☀️ at least 6 hours of sunlight daily. Mint can handle shade and grows like crazy once rooted. Don’t overcrowd — one cutting per jar is best. Also, if you're transplanting to soil, pinch the tips regularly to encourage bushy growth.

Kitchen Waste Farm Challenge

Kitchen Waste Mushroom Farm

Needs🧰/ Steps 🛠️:

  • Need 1: Used coffee grounds, damp cardboard, and mushroom spores OR a grow kit (oyster mushrooms = beginner-friendly)
  • Need 2: A bucket, cardboard box, or plastic tub.
  • Need 3: Spray bottle for misting.
  • Need 4: A dark, humid spot (under sink, kitchen cabinet, or closet).
  • Step 1: Layer coffee grounds/cardboard in your container.
  • Step 2: Add spores (from a kit or culture).
  • Step 3: Mist daily and keep in a dark, damp spot.
  • Step 4: Watch for growth in 1–2 weeks.
  • Step 5: Harvest & eat when fully grown (usually within 3–4 weeks).

Tips 💡Use a reusable container and compost any leftover substrate after harvesting. Share your learnings on growing food from waste!
